Coming from a small town in B.C., there was definitely not as much diversity as there is here in Edmonton. I'm still not used to seeing so many different faces and hearing so many different languages! Every time I hear people speaking another language, I think they are speaking English and I've just forgotten how to understand it for a second. My nationality is Canadian. I was born in Trail, B.C.- a small refinery town tucked away in the Kootenay valley. I lived there full time until July 2021! My ethnic background is mostly Scandinavian/Celtic with a bit of Italian and French Canadian. English is my mother language, and I am taking French lessons in school- none of my parents or grandparents speak any language besides English. I'm taking French because it is a part of my background, and I find the language really fascinating. I also spend a lot of my time researching my celtic background since I find paganism and it's broad range of customs (like druids), very interesting. I think it's really awesome that I come from such a fascinating line of people.
